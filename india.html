India's Progress to Achieve This Goal

Goal 4 - Quality Education

India has made significant progress towards achieving the Sustainable Development Goal (SDG) of quality education. The country has implemented several initiatives and policies to improve access to education, enhance the quality of education, and promote lifelong learning opportunities for all.

Some of the key initiatives and policies include:

Right to Education Act

The Right to Education Act was enacted in 2009, which mandates free and compulsory education for all children aged between 6 and 14 years.

Samagra Shiksha Abhiyan

The Samagra Shiksha Abhiyan was launched in 2018 to provide holistic education to children from pre-school to class 12.

National Education Policy 2020

The National Education Policy 2020 aims to transform the education system by focusing on holistic, multidisciplinary, and skill-based education.

Beti Bachao Beti Padhao

The Beti Bachao Beti Padhao initiative aims to promote education for girls and to address gender-based discrimination.

Digital India

The Digital India initiative aims to provide digital infrastructure and access to digital resources to enhance the quality of education.

Despite these initiatives, there are still significant challenges that need to be addressed to achieve the SDG of quality education in India. These challenges include improving the quality of education, reducing the dropout rate, promoting gender equality in education, and addressing the digital divide.
