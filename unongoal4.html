UN on Quality Education

Goal 4 - Quality Education

The United Nations (UN) recognizes the importance of quality education and has included it as one of the Sustainable Development Goals (SDG 4) adopted in 2015. According to the UN, quality education means an education that is:

Inclusive

Education should be available and accessible to all, regardless of their gender, ethnicity, religion, or socioeconomic status.

Relevant

Education should provide learners with the knowledge and skills they need to succeed in their lives, including in the workforce and in their communities.

Effective

Education should be of high quality, with trained and qualified teachers, appropriate infrastructure, and up-to-date curriculum and teaching methods.

Equitable

Education should be distributed fairly, ensuring that everyone has the same opportunities to access and benefit from education.

Empowering

Education should help learners to develop critical thinking skills, creativity, and a sense of agency, so that they can become active and engaged members of society.

The UN emphasizes that quality education is essential for achieving sustainable development, reducing poverty and inequality, promoting gender equality, and building peaceful and resilient societies. By investing in quality education, we can help to create a better future for all.
